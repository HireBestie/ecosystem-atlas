# Anthropic Startup Partnerships — Interview Prep

**Role:** [Startup Partnerships, France & Southern Europe](https://job-boards.greenhouse.io/anthropic/jobs/5131095008)  
**Standard:** Unsparing. Atlas ≠ ecosystem judgment. Relationships ≠ portfolio activation.

---

## 0. The honest scorecard

| Criterion | Current bar | What would close it |
|---|---|---|
| Deep FR/EU ecosystem + AI landscape | **Weak as *owned* knowledge.** Atlas/public research ≠ recall without notes. | Can answer “why Motier / Elaia / Kima / Station F?” cold, with thesis, stage, density, decision-maker, Claude angle. |
| Built VC/accelerator relationships that drove portfolio engagement | **Clearest gap.** No named counterpart + co-owned programme + measured activation + reference. | Run [NETWORK_ACTIVATION_PROTOCOL.md](./NETWORK_ACTIVATION_PROTOCOL.md): identify humans → map L1/L2 bridges → warm intros. One real receipt beats a prettier Atlas. |
| Technical peer credibility (Claude / Claude Code / evals) | **Strongest lane.** Builder product, architecture, live debugging. | Keep receipts public and demoable; practice live founder-debug. |
| Partnership / coalition execution | **Transferable (Open Diplomacy etc.).** | Translate into VC language: cohort design, reporting, renewal — without inventing VC history. |
| Day-1 operating system | **Demonstrated as method** (Mission Graph). | Say explicitly: “this is how I would run the mandate; it is not proof funds already trust me.” |

**Positioning line (use this, not fluff):**

> I can show builder credibility, partnership ops muscle, and an operating system for the mandate. I do **not** yet have a counterpart-validated record of activating French VC portfolios. My job in the next weeks is to earn that knowledge and those conversations — not to fake them.

## 1. What you must know *before* joining vs what the job teaches

### Must know before (cannot bluff)

- Top FR (+ ES/IT/PT first pass) funds: stage, cheque size band, AI thesis vs opportunistic AI, 3–5 portfolio names that matter for Claude.
- Accelerators/hubs that can actually mobilise technical founders (not just brand).
- Where Claude already has pull vs where Mistral / OpenAI / local “sovereign” framing wins.
- Concrete technical objections French builders raise (GDPR/residency, French output quality, eval/cost/latency, Claude Code enterprise friction).
- Difference between **ecosystem visibility** and **qualified activation**.

### Must *not* pretend to know (learn on the job / with Anthropic)

- Internal Anthropic partner tiers, credit economics, legal templates, CRM truth.
- Exact ROI definition Anthropic will use this quarter.
- Which product bets Product wants feedback on *this month*.
- Confidential deal status with any fund.

If asked something in the second bucket: say what you would instrument, who you would ask, and what leading indicators you’d watch. Do not invent.

## 2. Evidence bars (memorise)

### “I know the ecosystem” — five receipts

1. Differentiated map (AI-native vs opportunistic; stage; density; who decides).
2. Original falsifiable theses (can be wrong; have a resolve-by).
3. Technical peer credibility (demo, repo, live debug).
4. External recognition (invites, intros, teaching asks — *earned*, not claimed).
5. Insight → action (prioritisation / programme / content changed because of evidence).

### “I have VC relationships” — hierarchy

| Strength | Evidence |
|---|---|
| Weak | Events, LinkedIn, list of names |
| Moderate | Intro, spoke once, advised one founder |
| Strong | Co-designed programme with named VC/accelerator |
| Very strong | Measured portfolio activation |
| Exceptional | Renewed + reference from counterpart |

**Do not** upgrade Koondal fundraising or Bpifrance founder-side fluency into “I run VC portfolio programmes.”

## 4. Interview questions to prepare

### A. Relationship / portfolio activation (vulnerability — Q2–6)

1. Which five French funds would you prioritise in month one, and why that order?  
2. Who specifically do you already know at each?  
3. What have you built *together* with a VC or accelerator?  
4. How many portfolio companies participated in anything you ran?  
5. What founder behaviour changed after?  
6. Which founder or investor would take a reference call about you?  
7. Describe the last time a fund said no to you — why?  
8. Have you ever reported activation metrics to a GP or platform team?  
9. What’s your definition of “portfolio engagement” vs “event attendance”?  
10. Name one accelerator that can mobilise technical founders and one that mostly mobilises brand — how do you know?  
11. Walk me through how you’d get a meeting with Motier / Elaia / Kima next week without Anthropic’s logo.  
12. What would make you *deprioritise* a prestigious fund?  
13. How do you avoid becoming “the credits person”?  
14. Describe a coalition you built that is analogous to VC portfolio activation (Open Diplomacy etc.) — and where the analogy breaks.  
15. If we hired you tomorrow, what is the first *external* conversation you book, and what ask do you make?

### B. Ecosystem judgment (the knowledge gap — own it)

16. AI-native vs opportunistic AI investor — give three examples each in France.  
17. How does Paris differ from Barcelona / Madrid / Milan / Lisbon for AI founder density and distribution?  
18. Where is Claude already winning with FR builders, and where does it lose?  
19. When do French teams pick Mistral for sovereignty vs Claude for capability?  
20. What’s the real role of Station F for technical activation vs visibility?  
21. France Digitale vs La French Tech — different jobs in a partner motion?  
22. Which media/newsletters actually reach French AI builders?  
23. Name three FR AI infra / agent / vertical startups you’d put in a first cohort and why.  
24. What’s thin in your Southern EU map, and how would you close it in 30 days?  
25. Give one falsifiable ecosystem thesis and how you’d resolve it by a date.  
26. Credits: when helpful, when counterproductive?  
27. Evaluation methodology vs credits — which is the bigger production blocker today, and what evidence do you have?  
28. Developer communities vs VCs — who creates better technical champions vs faster distribution?  
29. French-language executive enablement vs English technical materials — who needs which?  
30. If a partner wants a big branded summit and you want a 12-company workshop, how do you negotiate?

### C. Technical peer credibility (strength — still drill live)

31. Demo a Claude / Claude Code workflow you’d show a founder in 10 minutes.  
32. How do you structure an evaluation harness for a B2B workflow product?  
33. Trade-offs: latency, cost, tool use, context window, structured outputs.  
34. How do you debug a flaky agent loop live with a founder?  
35. Security / data residency objections — what do you say that isn’t marketing?  
36. When is Claude Code the wrong recommendation?  
37. How would you teach “evals before demo day” to a portfolio cohort?  
38. What product feedback would you send to Anthropic Product after five Motier-like founders?  
39. Build vs buy for an internal activation dashboard — what do you build day one?  
40. Show something you shipped that a technical GP would respect.

### D. Programme design cases (practice out loud)

41. Design a 6-week evaluation cohort for Fund X (12–20 companies). Ownership, curriculum, office hours, reporting.  
42. Motier vs Elaia vs Kima — three different first offers.  
43. Accelerator residency: what you teach in one day vs four weeks.  
44. Counter-programme: credits-only pilot — why you refuse or constrain it.  
45. Measure adoption vs attendance for a workshop.  
46. Handle “we already tried Claude, didn’t stick.”  
47. Handle “we need EU-hosted / sovereign.”  
48. Handle GP wants logo; platform team wants activation.  
49. Kill criteria mid-cohort.  
50. Renewal conversation after cohort 1 — what numbers do you bring?

### E. Role / process / Anthropic-facing

51. How would your first 30 / 60 / 90 days look?  
52. How do you prioritise when every fund wants a meeting?  
53. How do you work with Sales, Product, DevRel, Legal?  
54. What should Anthropic *not* do in France in 2026?  
55. How will you know you’re failing by month three?  
56. What do you need from Anthropic to succeed that you can’t create alone?  
57. How do you stay honest when the Atlas or a thesis is wrong?  
58. Conflict: team wants visibility metrics; you want activation — how do you argue?  
59. What from your Mission Graph would you throw away after week one of real conversations?  
60. Why you, why this geography, why now — without overselling VC relationships?

### F. Questions *you* ask them

61. How do you currently define qualified activation for startup partnerships?  
62. Which VC/accelerator relationships already exist in FR/Southern EU, and where are the holes?  
63. What failed partner motions should I not repeat?  
64. How tightly is this role coupled to credits vs technical enablement?  
65. What product feedback loops actually reach Product today?  
66. What does success look like at 6 and 12 months for this seat?  
67. How much of the job is founder-facing technical work vs fund-platform relationship work?  
68. Who would I partner with weekly inside Anthropic?

## 6. Four-week learning sprint (knowledge make-up)

### Week 1 — Map until cold

- Complete comparison table for top 8 FR partners.  
- 10 dossiers.  
- Daily: pick one fund, answer the 7 cold questions aloud, record yourself.

### Week 2 — Theses + counters

- Write 5 falsifiable ecosystem theses with resolve-by dates.  
- For each: one supporting source, one counter-finding (audience intel / public threads).  
- Drill: credits vs evals; Mistral vs Claude; FR language quality.

### Week 3 — Conversations (relationship gap)

- 8–12 real conversations: ex-portfolio founders, operator friends, one warm intro toward a fund platform person if possible.  
- Log: what you believed → what you learned → what you changed.  
- Do **not** pitch Anthropic; pitch curiosity and usefulness.

### Week 4 — Programme + technical receipts

- Write one full cohort design (Motier-shaped) with metrics.  
- Rehearse 10-minute technical demo + live debug.  
- Mock interview: only section A + B questions until you stop hedging.

**Exit test:** A stranger names Motier, Elaia, Kima, Partech, Station F — you answer without notes. You can state your VC-relationship gap in one clean sentence.

## 7. How to talk about the Atlas in interview

**Use it for:** method, prioritisation logic, falsifiability, programme design, measurement, worldview counterfactuals.

**Do not use it for:** “I already know the French ecosystem,” or “funds trust me.”

Script:

> The Atlas is my homework and my operating sketch: entities, resolvable assumptions, rebuttals, and principle-governed recommendations. It shows how I would run map → prioritise → programme → enable → measure. It does not replace lived fund relationships or portfolio activation receipts — those I still need to earn.

---

## 8. Claim hygiene

| Claim type | Allowed only if |
|---|---|
| “I know X fund” | You can do the cold drill + cite sources |
| “I have a relationship with Y” | Named counterpart would confirm |
| “We activated N companies” | You can show who / what / metric / date |
| “I’m technical enough” | You can demo and debug live |
| “I’m ready day one” | Method + learning plan + honest gap |

If you cannot clear the bar, say the weaker true sentence.
